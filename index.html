Academic Summary

Primary Interests

My work focuses on advanced control systems, with a particular emphasis on nonlinear control, UAV performance, system identification, and contraction analysis. I aim to bridge rigorous theory with practical applications in complex engineering systems.

Education History

I am a Control Engineering lecturer and researcher with experience at Addis Ababa Science and Technology University and Debre Berhan University. I hold an MSc in Control Engineering and a BSc in Electrical Engineering (Control).

Control Systems

This course lays the foundation for understanding the principles of classical control systems, enabling students to model, analyze, and design feedback control systems for engineering applications. The course covers: Mathematical Modeling of Dynamic Systems, Transfer Functions, Block Diagrams and Signal Flow Graphs, Time-Domain Response Analysis, Transient and Steady-State Characteristics, Stability Analysis, Root Locus Method, Frequency Response Analysis, Bode and Nyquist Techniques, Performance Evaluation, Controller Design Using Time-Domain and Frequency-Domain Specifications, and Practical Applications of Linear Feedback Control Systems..

Modern Control Systems

This course introduces the fundamental principles of modern control systems using the state-space approach, providing students with the theoretical foundation for the analysis and design of linear dynamic systems. The course covers: State-Space Representation of Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) Systems, Mathematical Modeling of Dynamic Systems, Conversion Between Transfer Function and State-Space Models, Solution of State Equations, Eigenvalue and Eigenvector Analysis, Similarity Transformations, Phase-Plane Analysis, Controllability and Observability, Kalman Decomposition, State Feedback and Pole Placement, Reference Tracking and Servo-System Design, State Estimation and Observer Design, Kalman Filtering, Optimal Control and Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) Design, Performance Indices, Multi-Input Multi-Output (MIMO) Systems, Introduction to Nonlinear Systems, and Analog Simulation of Dynamic Systems. Emphasis is placed on the analysis, design, and implementation of modern control techniques for engineering applications.

3
Digital Control Systems

The course deals with the theory on the design of digital control systems and their implementation. Major topics include: State-space system model. Discrete-time signals and systems; z-transform. Sampling: the ideal sampler, data reconstruction, quantization effects. Discrete equivalents to continuous-time transfer functions. Stability analysis: Jury's stability test; root locus; Nyquist stability criterion. Design of digital control systems: transform techniques; stat-space techniques. Hardware and software aspects in implementation. Laboratory work will include experiments on PID controller, and state feedback controller design of an electro-mechanical system.

Electromagnetic Field

This course lays the foundation for understanding basic electromagnetic concepts, fundamental governing laws and guiding principles which students can apply in subsequent courses. The course covers: Vector Algebra, Scalar and Vector Fields, Coordinate Systems and Transformation, Vector Calculus, Line, Surface and Volume Integrals, Vector Differential Operator, Electrostatic fields, Coulomb’s and Gauss’s Laws for static electric charge distributions in vacuum and material bodies, Electrostatic Boundary Value Problems, Poisson’s and Laplace’s equations, Resistance and Capacitance, Magnetostatic Fields, BiotSavart’s and Ampere’s Laws for steady state current source in free and material space, Magnetic Forces, Materials, and Devices, Faraday’s Law, Maxwell’s Equations for Static and dynamic (time-varying) Electromagnetic Fields.
